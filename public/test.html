REST API Hosted Payment Page (HPP) Simply Connect

Nuvei APM REST API — Integration Test Console

Each step below fires one specific Nuvei call and shows the exact request/response next to it. Sequence: (1) /getSessionToken authenticates and returns a short-lived sessionToken — no order exists yet — (2) pick an APM and fill its fields, (3) /payment submits the charge (immediate result for direct-flow APMs like BLIK, or a redirectUrl for redirect-flow APMs), (4) the async DMN webhook delivers the final authoritative status, (5) optionally cross-check on demand with /getPaymentStatus or /getTransactionDetails, and (6) manage the money afterward with /settleTransaction (capture), /voidTransaction, or /refundTransaction.

Step 1Get session token

Calls /getSessionToken to authenticate and obtain a sessionToken (valid ~10 minutes). Takes no amount/currency at all — only merchantId, merchantSiteId, and merchantSecretKey, all read server-side from .env, plus a generated clientRequestId. Amount/currency are collected later, in Step 3, since that's the only call whose checksum actually uses them.

Step 2Select APM, customer details & amount

Choosing an APM loads its description, flow type, and mandatory fields. This step doesn't call Nuvei directly — everything collected here (including amount/currency) is sent together as the Step 3 /payment request.

No direct Nuvei call in this step — the fields collected here are sent as part of the Step 3 /payment request.

Step 3Initiate payment

Calls /payment with paymentOption.alternativePaymentMethod, passing the sessionToken obtained in Step 1 and the amount/currency/customer details entered in Step 2 — this is the request whose checksum actually includes amount/currency (see §1.3 of the solution design doc). Redirect-flow APMs return a redirectUrl here instead of an immediate APPROVED/DECLINED.

Step 5Verify & inspect

/getPaymentStatus only works while the session is open and shouldn't be polled repeatedly (risk of IP block) — prefer the DMN for routine confirmation. /getTransactionDetails is the durable lookup by transactionId, usable after the session has expired.

Step 6Post-payment: capture / void / refund

Capture (/settleTransaction) only applies if the original /payment was an Auth-type transaction — most direct-settle APMs will reject it. Void only works in the brief window before settlement. Refund applies to already-settled transactions and is the most common post-payment op for APMs.
